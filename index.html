Dr. Dimas Danar Dewa
Urban Systems & Spatial Modeling
Modeling Urban–Environment Dynamics for Climate-Resilient and Sustainable Development

I am an Urban Studies Researcher and Spatial Data Analyst with a specialization in advanced spatial analysis, GIScience, remote sensing, and environmental risk assessment.

My work integrates spatial modeling, geospatial technologies, and urban environmental studies to address complex sustainability challenges in rapidly growing cities.

My research focuses on three core thematic areas that support the development of sustainable, low-carbon, and climate-resilient urban environments:

• Spatial analysis and modeling of urban–environment systems, examining how land-use dynamics, urban form, and socio-environmental processes interact.

• Integration of GIS, remote sensing, entropy modeling, and geospatial big data for evidence-based urban management.

• Strengthening urban resilience through disaster risk reduction, adaptive planning, and climate-responsive strategies.
