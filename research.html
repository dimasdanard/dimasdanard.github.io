Research Output

My research investigates urban–environment systems through spatial modeling, GIScience, and remote sensing. The roadmap below illustrates the evolution of my research trajectory, starting from the development of spatial indicators for urban morphology and land-use dynamics toward integrated modeling of urban climate systems, ecological processes, and spatial data science. Early work (2021–2024) focused on developing spatial metrics to analyze urban spatial structure, land-use transformation, and urban morphology. More recent studies (2025–2026) extend these approaches toward modeling urban environmental systems, including urban climate mitigation, ecological corridor analysis, and geospatial big-data analytics. This trajectory ultimately supports my long-term research vision toward urban systems modeling for climate-resilient and low-carbon development.

Future Research Vision
2030
Urban systems modeling for climate-resilient and low-carbon development, integrating ecological networks, spatial big-data analytics, and climate-risk assessment.
